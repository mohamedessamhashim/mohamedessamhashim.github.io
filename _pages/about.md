I am a Medical AI Researcher and Physician (M.B.,B.S.) with a passion for leveraging artificial intelligence to solve complex clinical challenges, particularly in neuro-oncology and neurodegenerative diseases.

I recently earned my Bachelor of Medicine, Bachelor of Surgery (M.B.,B.S.) from the University of Medical Sciences & Technology (UMST) in Khartoum, Sudan, graduating with Distinction/First-Class Honours. I will be joining University Hospitals Cleveland Medical Center as a Research Fellow, focusing on Quantitative MRI in Neurological Diseases.

My research spans across advanced medical imaging and epidemiological modeling. As a Global Burden of Disease Collaborator with IHME at the University of Washington, I contribute to evaluating neurological disease metrics and neuro-oncology burden across North Africa and the Middle East. Additionally, as a Medical AI Research Collaborator at the Kann Lab (MGB/Harvard Medical School), I develop LLM-assisted segmentation pipelines for pediatric gliomas (BraTS-PEDs).

In the startup ecosystem, I founded Craniolabs, a medical AI startup aimed at solving data bottlenecks in low-resource hospitals. Through this venture, we built clinical data pipelines for over 300 CT and MRI scans to empower underserved communities, a mission supported by the NVIDIA Inception program.

My technical toolkit includes Python (PyTorch, MONAI, HuggingFace), R, and deep learning architectures (nnU-Net, Swin-UNETR), coupled with strong clinical foundations such as NIHSS certification and direct patient care experience in diverse environments across Egypt, Tanzania, and Sudan.

Please feel free to explore my portfolio and publications, or reach out to connect!
